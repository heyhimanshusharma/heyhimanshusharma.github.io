Favorite Books
"Books break the shackles of time. A book is proof that humans are capable of working magic." - Carl Sagan
The Selfish Gene
Richard Dawkins
A revolutionary look at evolution from the gene's perspective. This book fundamentally changed how I view life and inspired my interest in biological machines.
"We are survival machines—robot vehicles blindly programmed to preserve the selfish molecules known as genes."
Cosmos
Carl Sagan
My gateway into astronomy and scientific thinking. Sagan's poetic approach to science continues to influence how I communicate complex ideas.
"We are made of star stuff."
The Red Queen
Matt Ridley
A fascinating exploration of theory of evolution and how humans are in an arms race with parasites, viruses predators, or rival suitors. Human nature, Ridley argues, is deeply shaped by the evolutionary need to reproduce successfully.
"In history and in evolution, progress is always a futile, Sisyphean struggle to stay in the same relative place by getting ever better at things.”
How Innovation Works
Matt Ridley
Matt Ridley by keeping evolution as his central theme for writing explains why free trade is crucial for accelerating progress.
"innovation is organic because it must be a response to an authentic and free desire, not what somebody in authority”.
