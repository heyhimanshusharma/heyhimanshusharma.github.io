Favorite Books
Books that have shaped my thinking and continue to inspire my work in science and engineering.
THE SELFISH GENE
The Selfish Gene
Richard Dawkins
A revolutionary look at evolution from the gene's perspective. This book fundamentally changed how I view life and inspired my interest in biological machines.
"We are survival machines—robot vehicles blindly programmed to preserve the selfish molecules known as genes."
SURELY YOU'RE JOKING
Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!
Richard Feynman
Feynman's curiosity and approach to understanding the world deeply resonates with my own quest for knowledge across disciplines.
"I have to understand the world, you see."
BIOMIMICRY
Biomimicry: Innovation Inspired by Nature
Janine Benyus
The definitive guide to learning from nature's designs. Essential reading for anyone interested in sustainable innovation and evolutionary design.
"Nature as model, nature as measure, nature as mentor."
COSMOS
Cosmos
Carl Sagan
My gateway into astronomy and scientific thinking. Sagan's poetic approach to science continues to influence how I communicate complex ideas.
"We are made of star stuff."
THE ELEGANT UNIVERSE
The Elegant Universe
Brian Greene
A fascinating exploration of string theory and the nature of reality. Greene's ability to make complex physics accessible is truly remarkable.
"The boldness of asking deep questions may require unforeseen flexibility if we are to accept the answers."
GÖDEL ESCHER BACH
Gödel, Escher, Bach
Douglas Hofstadter
An eternal golden braid connecting mathematics, art, and consciousness. This book opened my eyes to the beautiful patterns underlying all of existence.
"In the end, we are self-perceiving, self-inventing, locked-in mirages that are little miracles of self-reference."
